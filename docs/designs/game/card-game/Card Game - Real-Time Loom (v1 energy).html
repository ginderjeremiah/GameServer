What makes it weird

  • The present is a moving deadline. Strikes resolve the instant they touch NOW — you don't get a turn, you get a window.
  • Cards are spans, not points. A Guard isn't "block 8," it's "block for these 7 ticks." You commit to when, then live with it as it scrolls in.
  • Lead time = the whole game. Long, strong cards (Channel) must be laid far ahead and protected; cheap ones (Dodge) are last-second saves.
  • Reflex is your escape hatch. Slow time to read a nasty pattern — but the reserve drains, so panicking has a cost. (Straight out of Second Second.)
  • Still your engine. The 40ms tick is the literal grid; the boss's intents are pinned to ticks exactly like a real battle.

Re-thought attributes power tempo

Split so a specialised build never breaks the loom. The skill of play lives in tempo stats everyone owns; power only flavours offense.

STR / INT Offense scales off whichever is HIGHER. Pure-Strength and pure-Intellect builds hit equally — STR paints your basics red & physical, INT paints them purple & arcane. You're never starved for not having both.
AgilityReflex reserve — how much you can slow time. Also trims card cast-span (snappier).
DexterityHand size, draw rate & placement snap. Your throughput on the board.
EnduranceBlock spans last longer & absorb more · HP · channels harder to interrupt.
LuckSpawns glowing crit ticks on the board — resolve a hit there to double it. Better draws.

Open forks — let's decide

  • Default tempo: ship it as twitchy Continuous, gentle Beat (auto-pauses to plan), or Step (you advance time)? Try all three above — which fits an "active break" in an idle game?
  • Drop point: cards currently appear just right of NOW and scroll in. Should you instead place them anywhere on the visible future (more chess), or always at NOW (more reflex)?
  • Interrupts: should an unblocked hit cancel a Channel mid-cast (high risk/reward), or just chip your HP?
  • How long is the board you can see? Dexterity-gated foresight, or fixed?

The card vocabulary (starter)

  • Guard — wide block span, your bread-and-butter defense (Endurance ↑ width).
  • Dodge — tiny, cheap block; a precise last-second negate (Agility ↑ window).
  • Slash — short windup → strike. Your build's basic damage (max STR/INT).
  • Channel skill — long windup, big payoff, interruptible. Skills = rare cards seeded in.
