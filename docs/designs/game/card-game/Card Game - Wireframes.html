multi-resource economy

The Six Wells

Forget a single "Energy" bar. Each turn you refill six attribute pools and cards cost specific colors. Your character build literally is your economy — a Strength build floods red, an Intellect build floods purple. The puzzle is juggling six curves at once.

  • 1Six Wells refill from your Attributes every turn. STR/END/INT/AGI/DEX/LCK each give pips equal to (a fraction of) that stat.
  • 2Cards cost colored pips, not generic energy. "Cleave" wants 2 STR; "Fire Bolt" (a Skill) wants 2 INT. Off-build cards are playable but starve you.
  • 3Bosses attack your economy — drain, lock, or tax a Well — forcing you to improvise off your comfort colors.
  • 4Relics = equipped items. They bend Well refills or let you convert one color into another.

timing & initiative puzzle

The Initiative Loom

Leans straight into your engine's DNA — the battle sim already runs on 40ms ticks. So instead of rigid "my turn / your turn," you place cards onto a shared timeline. The enemy's blows are already pinned to future ticks; you weave your attacks, blocks, and dodges in the gaps. Speed is everything.

  • 1You can see ahead a number of ticks set by Dexterity — more foresight = read more of the enemy's plan.
  • 2Card "speed" (lower = sooner) is reduced by Agility. A fast block can land before the enemy's strike on t3; a slow one arrives too late.
  • 3Enemy intents are pinned to ticks (red). Bosses chain combos, leave bait windows, or speed up as they enrage.
  • 4Skills are powerful but slow — high-impact cards you must set up several ticks in advance and protect.

spatial / positional deckbuilding

The Mannequin

Cards aren't just played — they're equipped to body slots on a paper-doll of your character. Where a card goes changes what it does: a damage card in the weapon slot strikes; the same card on the head becomes a counter. Your actual equipped items & mods seed which slots start powered. Build is literal and spatial.

  • 1Six equipment slots (head, body, weapon, off-hand, legs, trinket). Each reframes a card's effect by its slot's "verb."
  • 2Skills snap to thematic slots — Fire Bolt only fits a weapon/focus slot, so loadout matters before the fight even starts.
  • 3Bosses break your gear — disable a slot, force-discard what's equipped there, or overload it.
  • 4Equipped items = passive slot relics. Your real inventory loadout literally pre-arms the doll.

push-your-luck / risk ladder

The Ascent

The genuinely strange one. It's a press-your-luck climb: flip fate cards to ascend rungs, each worth more loot — but bust and you tumble. Your hand isn't attacks, it's risk-manipulation: hedge the odds, insure your winnings, peek ahead. Luck bends the deck itself. You decide when it ends — cash out for a quick win or push toward the boss rung.

  • 1The ladder escalates reward per rung. Higher = exponentially better loot, exponentially riskier flips.
  • 2Luck literally reshuffles fate — fewer bust cards, better peeks. The one attribute most players ignore becomes a headliner.
  • 3Hand = mitigation, not damage. Spend cards to soften busts, lock partial winnings, or force a re-flip.
  • 4You choose the length. Cash out = snackable. Push to the Warden's rung = a tense long haul. Variable by design.
