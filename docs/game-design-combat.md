# Combat Mechanics Detail тАФ Crit, Dodge, Reflection, Parry/Riposte

Deep-dive companion to [game-design.md ┬з Critical hits, dodge, parry & damage reflection](./game-design.md#critical-hits-dodge-parry--damage-reflection): the full rationale, derivations, and statistic/training-signal wiring for the opt-in combat variance mechanics. The main doc carries the summary; the exact coefficients live in `StaticAttributeModifiers` and the admin Workbench.

## Crit & dodge (opt-in)

**Crit is a per-skill opt-in** (crit rework [#1425](https://github.com/ginderjeremiah/GameServer/issues/1425), per-skill base [#1453](https://github.com/ginderjeremiah/GameServer/issues/1453)): each `Skill` carries its own `CriticalChance` тАФ a raw base probability, authored like `BaseDamage` and defaulting to `0` (never crits unless a designer opts it in). The battler's `CriticalChanceMultiplier` attribute only **scales** that per-skill base (`skill.CriticalChance ├Ч CriticalChanceMultiplier`) тАФ it carries a base of `1` (like `CooldownRecovery`/`CriticalDamage`) plus a `Luck` derivation ([#1525](https://github.com/ginderjeremiah/GameServer/issues/1525) тАФ LUK is the **proc-payoff amplifier**, spike [#1426](./spikes/1426-attribute-identity-sourcing.md)), so a committed skill still crits at its own authored rate with zero further investment while `Luck`/proficiency/gear bonuses raise the *multiplier* rather than flat chance. Because the multiplier scales a `0`-based enabler (`0 ├Ч mult = 0`), the opt-in holds by construction: LUK is dormant, not dead, until a crit-authored skill is fielded, and `Dexterity` deliberately gets **no** crit hook (it would double-dip тАФ damage scaling plus crit scaling тАФ and dominate STR for crit-committed physical builds). `CriticalDamage` keeps its `Luck` derivation and `1.5` base: inert without a crit, it pays off the moment a skill with an authored chance fires.

**Dodge is opt-in on the same template as parry** (dodge rework [#1523](https://github.com/ginderjeremiah/GameServer/issues/1523), spike [#1426](./spikes/1426-attribute-identity-sourcing.md)): `DodgeChance` is a decimal probability, base `0` and **authored-only** тАФ granted by items, item-mods, or skill effects, never derived from a core attribute тАФ so an un-enabled build sits at exactly `0` dodge. The battler's `DodgeChanceMultiplier` attribute only **scales** that authored chance (`DodgeChance ├Ч DodgeChanceMultiplier`, checked at the same site as parry) тАФ it carries a base of `1` plus an `Agility` derivation (AGI is the **tempo & evasion amplifier**), so `Agility` stays dormant until a dodge-granting item/effect is fielded, and the Evasion proficiency path's bonuses target the multiplier the same way Riposte targets `ParryChanceMultiplier`. Items, item-mods, and skill-effects can grant `DodgeChance`/`CriticalChanceMultiplier` through the normal modifier path; the coefficients are a conservative strawman expected to be tuned during balancing.

Crit/dodge outcomes surface to the player in the combat log, and `CriticalChanceMultiplier`/`CriticalDamage`/`DodgeChance`/`DodgeChanceMultiplier` appear on the attribute-breakdown screen, rendered with the percentage convention (`CriticalDamage`'s base-`1.5` multiplier reads as `150%`). The outcomes are tallied into player **statistics** (crit/dodge counts, the crit damage dealt, and the post-mitigation damage a dodge avoided) тАФ accumulated in the battle simulation and recorded server-side at battle completion like the other damage statistics. The player-facing **crit damage dealt** statistic is the *actual* full crit damage; the **Precision proficiency** trains on a distinct signal тАФ the crit overlay's uniform share claim (each crit hit books its landed, health-capped damage ├Ч `╧Ж(investment)`, #1481 тАФ see [game-design.md тЖТ Proficiency XP](./game-design.md#proficiency-xp--effect-based-power-normalized-accrual)) тАФ so the displayed statistic stays intuitive while the training signal stays proportional to crit investment.

## Damage reflection

**Damage reflection** (spike [#1330](./spikes/1330-mitigation-rework.md)) replaced the old **Block** mechanic тАФ a randomized flat reduction that was redundant with the Toughness curve and *anti-tank* (tanks want low variance, not RNG defence). It is the tank's **deterministic kill-condition**: on a **direct hit**, after mitigation, the defender deals `net ├Ч DamageReflection` back to the attacker, **bypassing the attacker's own mitigation**. It is deterministic (no proc), scoped to direct hits (DoT is never reflected тАФ there is no coherent attacker for a tick), and applies in **both** directions wherever it is granted. `DamageReflection` is **authored-only** тАФ granted by gear / item-mods / proficiency / class, never derived from a core attribute тАФ so it is a deliberate build identity with an opportunity cost rather than a stat tax (and an enemy reflects only where a designer authors it, a free boss-design seam). Because it self-scales with the size of the hit it returns, it is strongest exactly against the hard-hitters a tank is built to absorb, converting a pure tank's otherwise-inevitable timeout draw into a win path. Its magnitude is a strawman to tune during balancing; reflected damage is booked into the player's damage-dealt / damage-taken totals.

## Parry / Riposte

**Parry / Riposte** ([#1457](https://github.com/ginderjeremiah/GameServer/issues/1457)) is the opt-in avoid-and-counter delivery archetype тАФ the duelist's corner. An incoming **direct hit** can be **parried**: fully negated like a dodge, then answered with a **riposte** тАФ the equipped weapon's signature skill (the virtual fists' punch bare-handed, resolved once at battler assembly like the weapon-match gate) fired as a **first-class player hit** through the shared damage pipeline. Amplification, the signature's own authored `CriticalChance` (├Ч `CriticalChanceMultiplier`), the execute multiplier, the overlay share claims, and the enemy's reflection of the counter therefore all apply exactly as on a deliberate fire; the phantom fire is damage-only (no effects, no cooldown, no per-skill use attribution). `ParryChance` is a decimal probability, base 0 and **authored-only** тАФ granted by skill effects (a timed parry-stance window) or items, never derived тАФ scaled by the base-1 `ParryChanceMultiplier` that `Luck` derives into ([#1525](https://github.com/ginderjeremiah/GameServer/issues/1525)) and the **Riposte** proficiency path grants bonuses to (`0 ├Ч mult = 0`, inert until opted in, the same commitment template as crit). Parry is checked **before** dodge (a parry takes precedence, so dodge investment never starves the riposte) and never applies to DoT (no coherent attacker тАФ mirroring dodge/reflection). The counter books into the damage totals and the typed offense book (a sword riposte trains Swordsmanship), plus a dedicated **counter damage dealt** signal the Riposte path trains on **directly** тАФ like Retribution, not an overlay share, since the counter is new damage rather than a rider on a hit that would have landed anyway. Outcomes surface as the attacks-parried / damage-parried / counter-damage-dealt statistics, combat-log lines, and floaters. Deliberately, the counter has no damage knob of its own тАФ the weapon signature's tuning is the one "how good is this weapon's strike" lever, so better weapons upgrade the riposte automatically.

**Authoring watch-point:** effect re-application stacks magnitudes under a shared reset expiry, so a stance authored with duration > cooldown ramps `ParryChance` toward permanence тАФ author duration тЙд cooldown unless the ramp is intended.
